The project began with the raw .glb model of the Rhomaleosaurus, which was rigged and animated manually in Blender. The armature consisted of bones mapped to the flippers and body to simulate swimming. The animation was exported alongside the geometry using Blender‚Äôs GLTF/GLB exporter, ensuring WebXR compatibility.

üåê Stage 2 ‚Äì A-Frame WebXR Viewer

An A-Frame viewer (index5.html) was developed as a VR-style experience that allowed users to orbit around the animated model. Custom lighting, a black-to-gray gradient background, and a floating info panel made the experience feel like a museum installation.

The A-Frame viewer included a working desktop and mobile version and was later screen-recorded using Windows Game Bar to capture orbiting behavior and animation for submission and portfolio purposes.

üß™ Stage 3 ‚Äì AR Scene Exploration

The transition to AR introduced multiple new challenges. First, attempts were made to activate A-Frame‚Äôs webxr="optionalFeatures: hit-test" for marker-less AR. However, local server hosting on a Surface Pro caused firewall issues that prevented the mobile phone from reaching the computer‚Äôs IP. Multiple attempts using Python‚Äôs http.server were made, but required the command window to remain open and firewall exceptions to be allowed.

After resolving the network connection, the phone successfully loaded the scene ‚Äî but Safari did not display the AR cube icon because the model was in .glb format, and iOS requires .usdz for native AR.

Attempts to convert .glb to .usdz using online tools and Blender‚Äôs exporter didn‚Äôt work reliably on the iPhone. This led to a platform pivot.

üöÄ Stage 4 ‚Äì Final Deployment via MyWebAR

The final version of the AR scene was created using MyWebAR.com, a no-code browser-based WebAR builder that supports marker-less AR on both Android and iOS devices.

The animated .glb model was uploaded and placed in a marker-less WebAR project. Animation was preserved and looped successfully. The AR scene could then be accessed through:

A direct mobile link to the hosted scene

A QR code that opens the AR viewer

An iframe embed code that allows the scene to be included on any website

Multiple successful screenshots were taken from an iPhone to demonstrate the working AR scene with the animated model placed in real space.

A video titled AR Scene_Rhomaleosaurus Swims.mp4 was recorded using the iPhone's built-in screen recorder, showing the model floating and animating in AR.

‚úçÔ∏è Course Submission Components

The following written sections were prepared and submitted for XR Course 3 ‚Äì Honors Assignment 3:

Design Idea & Sketch
The concept was inspired by natural history exhibits, aiming to recreate a prehistoric sea creature and place it in both VR and AR. The AR version allowed real-time placement without markers, offering an immersive and educational experience.

Development Approach and Tools
Blender was used for modeling and animation. Unity was attempted for AR but proved unreliable for mobile. A-Frame was used for the WebXR viewer, and MyWebAR hosted the final marker-less AR version. The workflow included rigging, exporting to .glb, testing AR on multiple devices, and recording.

Envisioned AR/VR User Experience
The intended experience was for users to view the animated creature either in a virtual museum-like viewer or placed into their physical space via mobile camera. No UI was used ‚Äî instead, interaction occurred through physical movement around the model.

Summary of Key Interactions & Reflection
Key interaction was marker-less AR exploration. Reflection focused on toolchain pivots, firewall troubleshooting, WebAR hosting, and achieving presence through animation. A major insight was the power of pivoting tools without losing the core user experience.

Open Issues and Feedback Questions
Remaining questions included how to compress .glb models for WebAR and whether object interactions (e.g., tap to rotate) could be added easily. Peer feedback was welcomed on best practices for real-time AR model optimization and multi-platform deployment.
