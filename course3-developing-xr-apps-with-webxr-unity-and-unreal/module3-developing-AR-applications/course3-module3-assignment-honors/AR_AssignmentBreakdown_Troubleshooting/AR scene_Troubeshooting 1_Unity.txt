1. ü¶∑ Eyes and Teeth Not Attached in Unity

Issue:
Upon importing the .fbx model, the eyes and teeth appeared as separate objects and did not animate with the body.

Root Cause:
The eyes and teeth were not rigged or weight-painted to the armature in Blender. Even though they were part of the .fbx export, Unity saw them as independent mesh objects.

Failed Fix Attempts:

Dragging and dropping Dino_Skin_Mat directly onto each eye/teeth mesh in the Project folder (instead of Hierarchy).

Attempting to parent the meshes in Unity alone without rigging in Blender.

Reapplying animations and hoping Unity would "reconnect" bones (it didn‚Äôt).

Replacing prefabs and using "Override" features in Unity Inspector.

Lesson Learned:
Proper rigging must be handled in Blender‚ÄîUnity cannot correct poor bone/vertex group associations post-export.

2. üß† Model Rotation / Bone Twist in Unity

Issue:
Rigged models would import and immediately rotate incorrectly in Unity, with some bones distorted or axes flipped.

Root Cause:
Blender's default Z-up axis conflicts with Unity‚Äôs Y-up system. Armature and mesh orientation needed to be corrected before export.

Failed Fix Attempts:

Manually rotating the prefab in Unity.

Applying rotation to only the armature, not the mesh.

Exporting without applying transforms (Ctrl + A ‚Üí Apply All Transforms).

Lesson Learned:
Always apply rotation, scale, and location in Blender before export. Use Ctrl + A ‚Üí Apply All Transforms on mesh and armature.
